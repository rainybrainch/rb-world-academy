# French 120 Complete Curriculum Structure
## Full Outline & Topic Progression (A1-B2)

**Total**: 120 lessons across 4 CEFR levels  
**Format**: TypeScript objects integrating into courses.ts  
**Enrichment**: 8-12 vocabulary terms, 5-8 example sentences, 3-tier questions per lesson

---

## A1 LEVEL - BEGINNER (30 Lessons)

### Chapter 1: Foundations (Lessons 1-6)

#### Lesson 1: Les Salutations et Formules Polies
- **Theme**: Introduction & Politeness
- **Vocabulary (12 terms)**: Bonjour, Bonsoir, Au revoir, S'il vous plaît, Merci, De rien, Excusez-moi, Comment allez-vous?, Ça va?, Enchanté(e), Je m'appelle, Quelle est votre nom?
- **Key Focus**: Greeting rituals, politeness hierarchy (tu/vous distinction)
- **Cultural**: French value of politeness, La bise greeting custom
- **Native Notes**: Speed varies by region; Parisians use faster liaisons

#### Lesson 2: L'Alphabet et la Prononciation
- **Theme**: Alphabet & French Sounds
- **Vocabulary (12 terms)**: L'alphabet, La voyelle, La consonne, La prononciation, L'accent, L'accent aigu (é), L'accent grave (è), L'accent circonflexe (ê), Le tréma (ï), La cédille (ç), Muet, La liaison
- **Key Focus**: French alphabet (26 letters), pronunciation rules, silent letters
- **Cultural**: Académie française role in language standardization
- **Native Notes**: Liaison patterns, regional accent variations

#### Lesson 3: Les Nombres de 0 à 20
- **Theme**: Basic Counting
- **Vocabulary (20 terms)**: Zéro, Un/Une, Deux, Trois, Quatre, Cinq, Six, Sept, Huit, Neuf, Dix, Onze, Douze, Treize, Quatorze, Quinze, Seize, Dix-sept, Dix-huit, Dix-neuf, Vingt
- **Key Focus**: Counting patterns, gender agreement (un/une)
- **Cultural**: French counting system differs from English (Belgian/Swiss variations)
- **Native Notes**: Rapid number sequences in conversation

#### Lesson 4: Les Jours et Les Mois
- **Theme**: Calendar & Time Concepts
- **Vocabulary (19 terms)**: Lundi, Mardi, Mercredi, Jeudi, Vendredi, Samedi, Dimanche, Janvier, Février, Mars, Avril, Mai, Juin, Juillet, Août, Septembre, Octobre, Novembre, Décembre, La semaine
- **Key Focus**: Days (starting Monday), months, seasonal associations
- **Cultural**: French week structure, national holidays, cultural significance of dates
- **Native Notes**: School calendar (vacances scolaires) central to life planning

#### Lesson 5: Les Couleurs
- **Theme**: Color Vocabulary
- **Vocabulary (12 terms)**: Rouge, Bleu, Blanc, Noir, Jaune, Vert, Orange, Rose, Violet, Gris, Marron, Doré
- **Key Focus**: Basic colors, gender/number agreement with adjectives
- **Cultural**: Symbolism (French flag colors), fashion influence
- **Native Notes**: Neutral color preferences in French fashion

#### Lesson 6: La Famille
- **Theme**: Family Members & Relationships
- **Vocabulary (20 terms)**: La famille, Le père, La mère, Le frère, La sœur, Le fils, La fille, Le grand-père, La grand-mère, L'oncle, La tante, Le cousin, La cousine, Le mari, La femme, L'enfant, Le bébé, Les parents, Les enfants, L'aîné
- **Key Focus**: Family structure, kinship terms, informal variations
- **Cultural**: Importance of family gatherings, multi-generational involvement
- **Native Notes**: Regional family traditions vary significantly

- **Key Focus**: Formal vs. informal registers, conditional politeness
- **Cultural**: Politeness hierarchy crucial in French society

#### Lesson 25: Révision A1 (A1 Review & Consolidation)
- **Theme**: A1 Level Comprehensive Review
- **Vocabulary**: All 280+ A1 terms

## A2 LEVEL - ELEMENTARY (30 Lessons)

### Chapter 1: Foundations of Narrative (Lessons 26-31)

- **Key Focus**: Formation of passé composé with avoir/être
- **Grammar Deep Dive**: Auxiliary verbs, past participle agreement
- **Cultural**: How French speakers narrate past events
- **Native Notes**: Spoken French relies heavily on passé composé vs. imparfait distinction

#### Lesson 27: Les Descriptions Physiques (Physical Descriptions)
- **Theme**: Describing People & Appearance
- **Vocabulary (12 terms)**: Grand, Petit, Mince, Gros, Beau, Joli, Laid, Les cheveux blonds, Les cheveux bruns, Les yeux bleus, Les yeux marron, Les yeux verts
- **Key Focus**: Adjective agreement, comparing people
- **Cultural**: French beauty standards and aesthetic discussions
- **Native Notes**: Compliments vary by context; not always appreciated as in English

#### Lesson 28: Les Émotions et les Sentiments (Emotions & Feelings)
- **Theme**: Expressing Emotional States
- **Vocabulary (12 terms)**: Heureux, Triste, Joyeux, Fâché, Peur, Amour, Colère, Jaloux, Fier, Honteux, Fatigué, Énervé
- **Key Focus**: Adjectives for emotions, expressing how you feel
- **Cultural**: French expressiveness, emotional openness varies
- **Native Notes**: Body language important in conveying emotions

#### Lesson 29: La Personnalité (Personality Traits)
- **Theme**: Character & Personality Description
- **Vocabulary (12 terms)**: Sympathique, Intelligent, Stupide, Gentil, Méchant, Courageux, Timide, Honnête, Malhonnête, Amusant, Ennuyeux, Drôle
- **Key Focus**: Personality adjectives, social interactions
- **Cultural**: How French culture describes people, social stereotypes
- **Native Notes**: Directness in describing others; honesty valued

#### Lesson 30: Les Relations Sociales (Social Relationships)
- **Theme**: Types of Relationships & Social Bonds
- **Vocabulary (12 terms)**: L'amitié, L'amour, Le mariage, L'amoureux, L'ami, La petite amie, Le petit ami, Le collègue, Le voisin, Le camarade, Proche, Éloigné
- **Key Focus**: Relationship vocabulary, social structures
- **Cultural**: French attitudes toward relationships, friendship depth
- **Native Notes**: Friendship more formal/slower to develop than in English cultures

#### Lesson 31: L'Imparfait (Imperfect Tense)
- **Theme**: Habitual/Continuous Past Actions
- **Vocabulary (10 terms)**: Habituellement, Régulièrement, Souvent, Toujours, Quand j'étais enfant, Je faisais, J'avais, Je voyais, L'imparfait, Répétion
- **Key Focus**: Imperfect tense formation and use
- **Grammar Deep Dive**: Contrast between passé composé and imparfait
- **Cultural**: Storytelling style uses both past tenses
- **Native Notes**: Native speakers switch between tenses effortlessly in narratives

## B1 LEVEL - INTERMEDIATE (30 Lessons)

### Chapter 1: Complex Narrative (Lessons 56-61)

- **Key Focus**: Pluperfect formation and use
- **Grammar Deep Dive**: Sequencing multiple past events
- **Cultural**: Sophisticated narrative structure
- **Native Notes**: Less common in speech; more formal register

#### Lesson 57: Le Futur Proche et Futur Simple (Future Tenses)
- **Theme**: Expressing future plans and intentions
- **Vocabulary (11 terms)**: Aller + infinitif, Demain, Dans une semaine, Bientôt, Futur proche, Futur simple, Je vais, Je ferai, L'intention, Le projet, L'avenir
- **Key Focus**: Two ways to express future
- **Grammar Deep Dive**: Subtle difference between aller/infinitif and futur simple
- **Cultural**: How French speakers talk about future plans
- **Native Notes**: Aller + infinitif much more common in conversation

#### Lesson 58: Le Conditionnel (Conditional Mood)

- **Vocabulary (11 terms)**: Si j'avais, Je pourrais, Tu devrais, Je voudrais, Hypothèse, Irréalité, Politesse, Suggestion, Conseil, Rêve, Suggestion
- **Key Focus**: Conditional formation and uses
- **Grammar Deep Dive**: Conditional in if-clauses and politeness

- **Native Notes**: Native speakers use extensively for politeness

#### Lesson 59: Le Subjonctif (Subjunctive Mood - Introduction)

- **Grammar Deep Dive**: When subjunctive is required

#### Lesson 60: Les Conjonctions et Connecteurs (Conjunctions & Connectors)

- **Key Focus**: Sophisticated sentence structure
- **Grammar Deep Dive**: Discourse markers for coherence

#### Lesson 61: L'Hypothèse et la Réalité (Hypothesis vs. Reality)

- **Native Notes**: Hypothetical arguments common in discussion
